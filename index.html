ABOUT

In 2002, David J Perry created the font CARDO.The font was created with the intention of being a free and accessible typeface.The creator David J. Perry had a target audience. He created this font with “classicists, biblical scholars, medievalists, and linguists” specifically in mind.Cardo was inspired by the typography of the Renaissance printer Aldus Manutius.

AVAILABLE

across Adobe Fonts, Google Fonts, and Font Squirrel. Cardo was created as a large unicode font that is equipped for general type setting. It is a high-quality Old Style font. Cardo is available in Regular, Italic, and Bold.

PURPOSE

The main points behind the creation of Cardo is staying true to the old type faces of the renaissance period while being able to be read for a prolonged period of time.

INSPIRATION

Cardo was inspired by Aldus Manutius who was an italain Renaissance Printer.The design of Cardo is an interpretation of Manutius' works. Manutius was the founder of Aldine Press and would devote the last of his life to publishing and disseminating rare texts.Alongside Francesco Griffo, he created the first standardized italicized typeface.
